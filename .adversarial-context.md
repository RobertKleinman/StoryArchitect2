# Adversarial Review: Blind Research Model Comparison — Methodology v2

## What we built and tested
A blind test script (`scripts/research-blind-test.ts`) that feeds identical ResearchContracts to different LLM models for cultural_researcher, grounding_researcher, and divergence_explorer roles. Outputs are blinded (randomized A/B labels) and the human judges "which is better."

## Round 1 results (Claude Haiku vs Gemini Flash)
- 30 calls total (5 contracts × 3 roles × 2 models)
- Claude won cultural (3-2) and grounding (3-1-1tie)
- Gemini won divergence (4-0-1tie)
- Overall: Claude 6, Gemini 7, Tie 2
- Gemini was 2-3x faster on every call

## Problems the human judge identified
1. **Presentation was a wall of text.** 15 side-by-side comparisons in a .txt file was exhausting and hard to evaluate consistently. Judge fatigue likely affected later comparisons.
2. **Only 2 models tested.** We have 4+ fast-tier models (Haiku, Gemini Flash, Grok Fast, Grok 4.1). Pairwise testing is too slow.
3. **No structured rubric.** "Which is better" is subjective and may mean different things for different comparisons.

## Additional problems I (Claude) identified
4. **N=1 per contract/role/model.** Temperature variance could determine winners. No statistical confidence.
5. **Schema compliance not tracked.** Gemini returned `undefined` for some fields. Matters for production.
6. **Contracts were hand-crafted by me (Claude).** May inadvertently favor models that think like Claude.
7. **No cost tracking.** Input/output tokens not measured.

## What we want reviewed
1. **How should multi-model comparison results be presented** so a human can judge quickly without fatigue? Consider: scoring rubrics, summary tables, LLM-assisted pre-scoring, highlight reels of best/worst outputs.
2. **Is blind human review even the right approach for this?** Or should we use LLM-as-judge (e.g., have Sonnet score all outputs on a rubric) and only use human review for spot-checks?
3. **With 4-5 models, what's the fastest path to a confident recommendation?** Round-robin pairs? All models on same contracts and rank? Tournament bracket?
4. **How many contracts/runs do we need for signal vs noise?** Is 5 contracts enough? Do we need multiple runs per contract to control for temperature?
5. **Should speed be part of the scoring?** Gemini is 2-3x faster. These roles run during user think-time with a ~30s window.
6. **What structured criteria should be used?** Specificity? Mechanism depth? Story-usefulness? Surprise factor? Schema compliance?
