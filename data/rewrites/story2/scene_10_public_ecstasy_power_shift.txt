SCENE 10: "Pit Yield"
Setting: The Pit — full public ceremony. The quarterly challenge or a major imperial event.
Characters: Zephyr, Khalid (crowd, Emperor present but not speaking)
POV: Zephyr

CHARACTER REFERENCE:
  Zephyr: This is it. He has the evidence/information from scene 9. The underground is ready. Cassius and Erik have done their parts. All Zephyr has to do is use the public submission to deliver the final blow — whisper the evidence during the foot worship, force Khalid's hand, shift power.
  Khalid: Doesn't know what's coming. Or does he? After scene 9, does he suspect and go through with the public ceremony anyway — because he'd rather lose power than lose Zephyr? That would be devastating.

REVISED DIRECTION:
  The climax. Public foot worship becomes the weapon. The thing that was used to humiliate Zephyr becomes the tool he uses to win.

  Key beats:
  - Public ceremony. The pit. Thousands watching. Emperor present.
  - Khalid demands public worship — the ritual they've done before
  - Zephyr kneels. Begins the worship. The crowd expects the usual show.
  - During the act, Zephyr whispers — evidence, an ultimatum, whatever forces Khalid to yield political power
  - Khalid's reaction: does he rage? Go quiet? Accept it? The original plan says "mutual ecstasy and power inversion" — meaning both of them are caught in the moment, both affected
  - The power shifts. Khalid yields. But it's not clean.
  - The ending: Zephyr won. But what did it cost? The addiction is real. The feelings might be real. He used intimacy as a weapon against someone he's bonded to. Is he free? Or just a different kind of enslaved?

  The mirror:
  Scene 1 ended with Khalid's foot on Zephyr's face — "Mine now."
  Scene 10 should invert this. Zephyr at Khalid's feet by choice — but this time, Zephyr is the one in control. The foot worship looks the same to the crowd. But everything underneath has changed.

  Tone:
  Not triumphant. Bittersweet. Zephyr got what he wanted but lost something he didn't know he had. The Hector parallel: Hector was broken and broadcast. Zephyr used the breaking to break back. But does that make him whole?

  Questions to resolve:
  - What exactly is the evidence/leverage? Senate corruption? Khalid's illegal gene-editing? Arena fraud?
  - Does the Emperor react? Does the underground make a public move?
  - What happens after? Does Zephyr walk away? Stay? Does Khalid let him go?
  - Is there a final line between them — something that acknowledges what they had, twisted as it was?
  - Does the story end here or is there an epilogue implied?
